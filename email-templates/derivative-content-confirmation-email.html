Webinar 1

Executive Summary

In a webinar hosted by Mark Bornstein, VP of Marketing at ON24, he emphasized the importance of strategic planning and promotion for successful webinars. Bornstein highlighted the need to start with a clear goal and Key Performance Indicator (KPI) for each webinar, and to consider the content, delivery mechanism, level of interactivity, and post-webinar actions to achieve this KPI. He stressed the importance of personalization and segmentation in marketing, and the need for a diverse and creative promotional strategy. Bornstein also recommended a longer promotional cycle, utilizing artificial intelligence for content creation, and a multi-channel approach including email and social media. He debunked the myth of shorter webinars being more effective and encouraged continuous experimentation and modification of promotional strategies. The webinar concluded with the importance of post-live promotions, given that almost half of viewers watch webinars on demand.

Speakers

  • Tessa Grefenstette, Associate Director, Search & Evolution
  • Brian Adams, Developer, Mindtek

Key Takeaways

  1. Webinar KPI Achievement: Webinar planning should start with a clear goal and a Key Performance Indicator (KPI) that the webinar is intended to drive, such as appointments, website visits, enrollments, or certifications. The right content, delivery mechanism, level of interactivity, and post-webinar actions should be considered to achieve the KPI.
  2. Personalized Webinar Marketing: Personalization and segmentation are crucial in webinar marketing. Even when promoting a single event to a large group, it can be beneficial to engage them differently and speak to them in different ways.
  3. Effective Webinar Timeline: A well-planned timeline for the webinar is essential, with at least eight weeks from the beginning of planning to the live event. This timeline should include dates for design, content creation, and promotion, as well as a post-live strategy to drive results.
  4. Creative Promotional Strategies: Promotional strategies should be creative, intentional, and diversified beyond just email. Artificial intelligence can be used to make content creation faster and easier, and the webinar title should let the audience know that they will learn something from the event.
  5. Impactful Email Promotions: Email promotions should be concise, impactful, and personalized. They should include bullet points outlining the webinar content, a value statement, and multiple clickable links. The same email should not be sent repeatedly; instead, the message and format should be varied.
  6. Post-Webinar Promotions: Promotions should continue after the live webinar has ended, as almost half of viewers watch webinars on demand. This can be achieved by creating on-demand content hubs and using AI to create new content forms to drive engagement. Continuous experimentation, testing, analysis, and modification of promotional strategies are encouraged.

Key Quote

The Art and Science of Driving webinar registration is catching your prospects at a moment of receptivity with a message that resonates. You don't know where your audience member is going to be when they see that promotional tweet, when they see your promotional e-mail. And then even if they're in a great receptive place, does your message resonate with them? And that is a lot to ask. Things are changing fast and we need to rethink our promotional strategies.
